Marketing Strategy
Step 1: Load Your 7 Backend Keyword Fields

Your Listing score is capped at 14 until the 7 KDP backend keyword fields are confirmed. This is the single fastest action you can take to improve discoverability. “Poetry journal” alone gets 15,545 monthly searches on Amazon. Your title includes “guided poetry journal” which is working. The backend fields need to spread reach across adjacent buyer-intent searches to pull in more of the 16,000+ monthly searches in this niche.

✓
Target buyer-intent terms across all 7 fields
Use a different term in each field. Do not repeat variations of the same phrase. Lead with: “poetry journal for women,” “guided poetry journal,” “journal women guided,” “poetry writing journal,” “poetry prompt journal,” “emotional healing journal,” and “self-reflection journal for women.” Each field is a separate search path into your listing.
✓
Avoid generic terms that attract the wrong buyer
Terms like “poetry journal bulk” (4,145 searches) bring in buyers looking for wholesale quantities, not personal journaling. “Journal poetry” and “poetry notebook journal” are useful but lower volume. Prioritize the terms that describe a woman searching for healing and self-reflection content specifically.
✓
Review your description opening line
Your first sentence in the description should lead with the reader’s need, not the book’s title. A woman searching for an emotional healing journal wants to know this book speaks to her before she reads further. If your opening line starts with “Reflections: First Light is...” rewrite it to lead with her problem. That change alone improves conversion.
↓
Step 2: Build to 15 Reviews Before Running Ads

You have 3 written reviews and a perfect 5.0 rating. That is a stronger foundation than most books in this niche have at this stage. Reaching 15 gives your listing the social proof needed to convert ad traffic into paid sales. Your 5-star average means every new review adds to an already-excellent signal. Getting to 15 is the priority before any money goes into ads.

Your $29 paperback is priced 81% above the niche average of $15.99. At that premium, social proof matters more than for a $15 book. Aim for 25+ reviews before scaling ad spend. The conversion rate at 25 reviews will justify the price point and make the ad spend efficient.
✓
Run an ARC campaign via BookSirens or Hidden Gems
Both platforms connect authors with readers who opt in to receive advance review copies in exchange for honest feedback. The women’s emotional wellness and personal growth readership on these platforms is large and active. A well-presented campaign for this title could generate 15 to 30 reviews within 4 to 6 weeks. This is the fastest legitimate path to the review threshold needed for ads.
✓
Add a warm review request inside the book itself
Place a natural, genuine request mid-book and at the back. Many readers who loved the experience simply never think to leave a review without a prompt. A sincere ask at the right emotional moment (just after they have completed a journaling exercise) yields the best response rates. Do not ask for a specific star rating. Amazon’s terms prohibit this.
✓
Reach women’s wellness and poetry communities directly
Reddit’s r/poetry, r/journaling, and r/selfimprovement have large, engaged communities. Facebook groups for women’s healing circles and journaling practice are populated by exactly the readers this book is designed for. A genuine, non-promotional post sharing the book’s intent (not a sales pitch) often generates both community engagement and organic review activity.
↓
Step 3: Launch Amazon Ads for the Paperback

Ads move the Sales/mo bar. The books earning real revenue in this niche are almost certainly running Sponsored Products campaigns. At $29, your paperback royalty is approximately $9.07. A profitable ad campaign means spending less than $9.07 per sale. Start conservatively, prove the ACoS, then scale. Your 90-day benchmark is Words to Live By at 37 reviews and $179/month. The 12-month target is In poetry we say... at 373 reviews and $790/month.

✓
Start with Sponsored Products at $5 to $7/day
Target: “poetry journal for women,” “guided poetry journal,” and “emotional healing journal.” These reach buyers with clear paid-book intent rather than browsers looking for free content. Run for 14 days before reviewing performance. Do not increase budget until the campaign shows a profitable ACoS.
✓
Add Sponsored Display ads on competitor ASINs
Target the product pages of In poetry we say... (BSR 89,071, $790/mo) and Words to Live By (BSR 696,827, $179/mo). Buyers on those pages are already in a buying mindset for this type of book. A well-placed display ad intercepts that intent directly and converts at higher rates than cold keyword searches.
✓
Target ACoS below 30% for profitability at $29
At $29 paperback, your royalty is roughly $9.07 (KDP standard rate). Target spending under $9.07 per sale. An ACoS of 30% or below is profitable. Pause keywords with no sales after 14 days. Scale what converts. The 90-day goal is $179/month (Words to Live By benchmark with 37 reviews).

Book Portfolio Opportunity
A Series Compounds Discoverability
Two books by the same author cross-sell each other automatically. Amazon’s “customers also bought” system means every reader who buys Reflections: First Light will be shown the next title. Each new book in the Reflections series adds a new keyword footprint, a new category placement, and a new set of search rankings.
1
Reflections: Still Water. A Guided Poetry Journal for Grief, Loss, and Finding Peace
A natural sequel in the Reflections series. Grief journals are a high-demand, high-emotion purchase with strong buyer intent. The audience for grief and loss journaling is large, underserved by premium guided journals, and motivated by real emotional need. Direct cross-sell from First Light readers who are continuing their healing journey.
2
Reflections: New Dawn. A Guided Poetry Journal for Rebuilding After Difficult Times
Targets the recovery and resilience readership. Women searching for “journal for starting over” or “self-help journal for women going through change” represent a distinct buyer segment from the healing readership. Continuation of the series brand with a new entry point into adjacent search demand.
3
Words That Heal: A Daily Poetry Prompt Journal for Women
A standalone title targeting the “poetry prompt journal” keyword (4,261 monthly searches) and “poetry writing journal” (4,291 monthly searches). A daily-prompt structure broadens reach beyond the Reflections brand and captures buyers looking for structured writing practice rather than a themed emotional journey.

When to Start Planning the Second Title

Focus on Steps 1 to 4 first. Get reviews to 15, ads running, and the listing fully optimised before writing anything new. When your first title is generating consistent monthly revenue, the second title compounds that income without starting from zero.
Series naming is a commercial asset. The “Reflections” brand on the spine of multiple journals creates shelf recognition, repeat purchase behaviour, and stronger series rankings in Amazon’s algorithm. Two or three titles in the same series with consistent cover design consistently outperform standalone titles at equivalent review counts.
